## What Is Delivery Management Software?

Delivery management software helps businesses plan, track, and optimize their delivery operations.

Unlike on-demand delivery platforms (like Uber or Bolt) where you pay per trip, delivery management software helps you **manage your own riders and operations**.

### Core Features

Good delivery management software includes:

* **Route planning** — Optimize delivery routes to save time and fuel
* **Real-time tracking** — See where riders are and when deliveries will arrive
* **Order management** — Organize deliveries, assign to riders, track status
* **Customer notifications** — Automated SMS or WhatsApp updates
* **Analytics** — See performance, costs, and areas to improve
* **Proof of delivery** — Photos, signatures, customer confirmation

### Why It Matters for Kenyan SMEs

For businesses in Nairobi, Mombasa, Kisumu, and across Kenya:

* **Control costs** — Flat pricing instead of per-delivery fees
* **Own customer experience** — Your branding, your communication
* **Improve efficiency** — Better routes, fewer failed deliveries
* **Scale sustainably** — Grow without proportional cost increases
* **Build operations** — Create a delivery system, not just move parcels

## What Happened to Sendy?

Sendy was once Kenya's most popular delivery platform. Then it shut down.

For many SMEs, this was a wake-up call.

**The lessons from Sendy's closure**:

* Delivery is too important to fully outsource
* Per-delivery pricing doesn't scale well for SMEs
* Businesses need ownership over operations
* Relying on a marketplace creates dependency risk

Sendy helped prove demand for delivery solutions in Kenya. But it also showed that SMEs need **management tools**, not just marketplace access.

## How to Choose Delivery Management Software

### 1. Match Your Business Size

**1-3 riders**: Simple tools, easy setup, mobile-first
**4-10 riders**: Route optimization, performance tracking
**10+ riders**: Team management, advanced analytics

Start with what you need now, but choose software that can scale.

### 2. Consider Your Tech Comfort

If your team isn't technical:
* Look for simple interfaces
* Avoid complex enterprise tools
* Choose platforms with good support
* Test the mobile app (riders will use this)

### 3. Understand Pricing Models

**Per-delivery pricing** (Uber, Bolt):
* Works for low volumes
* Becomes expensive at scale
* Unpredictable costs

**Subscription pricing** (Roundi):
* Fixed monthly cost
* Predictable budgeting
* Cost per delivery decreases as you grow

**Enterprise pricing** (Leta):
* Custom quotes
* Higher base costs
* Designed for large teams

### 4. Check Local Support

Delivery in Nairobi is different from delivery in New York.

Look for software that:
* Understands Kenyan traffic patterns
* Works on local smartphone models
* Offers M-Pesa integration (if needed)
* Has local customer support
* Understands SME budgets

### 5. Evaluate Real-Time Tracking

Customers want to know where their order is.

Good tracking means:
* Live GPS location
* Accurate ETAs
* Customer-facing tracking pages
* SMS or WhatsApp notifications

This reduces "where's my order?" calls significantly.

## Common Questions About Delivery Management Software

### "Do I need riders, or does the software provide them?"

Delivery management software (like Roundi) helps you manage **your own riders**.

If you don't have riders, you'll need to hire them. But once you do, you'll have:
* Lower costs per delivery
* Better customer experience control
* Consistent service quality

On-demand platforms (Uber, Bolt) provide marketplace riders but with less control and higher costs at scale.

### "What if I only have one rider?"

Delivery management software works whether you have one rider or ten.

Even with one rider, you benefit from:
* Better route planning
* Customer tracking
* Delivery accountability
* Professional operation

As you grow, the software scales with you.
